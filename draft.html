FOR RENT

Tarbet Lodge is set back from the road in very quiet and peaceful surroundings where you often see wildlife including deer, otter, eagles and many other species of birds and marine life. There is a lot to offer on the Ardnamurchan Peninsula and the Lodge is a great base from which you can explore the area. You can go walking, kayaking, cycling, fishing, or just sightseeing. Ardnamurchan lighthouse is the most westerly point of the UK mainland and there are many secluded beaches to enjoy.

LOCATION

200 yards away you will find the Salen Jetty shop which has a good selection of food, drink and household items. There is also a grocery shop in Acharacle and one in Strontian. Within a ten mile radius there are excellent restaurants selling locally produced food. Ancient castles such as Castle Tioram are within easy driving distance. The Ariundle Oakwoods and lead mines are in nearby Strontian. For climbers and hill walkers there are hills ranging from easy walks to challenging mountains. For sea anglers the nearby Salen Marina has launching facilities for those wishing to bring their own boat. The area is famous for its kayaking and sea canoeing where marine life is abundant. It is also a favourite area for divers as the area is a marine protected zone, complete with kelp forests and great clarity of water. Nearby Glenfinnan is a must for all Harry Potter fans wishing to see both the viaduct and the Harry Potter steam train, the Jacobite Steam Train journey from Fort William to Mallaig is a great experience, even if you don't like Harry Potter it is a scenic must. Tobermory, known to children as Ballymory, is just a short ferry journey away and is famous for both its multicoloured houses and its seafood restaurants, it is also a base for whale watching tours. There is the Tobermory distillery to visit and there is also the Ardnamurchan distillery back on the Peninsula ten minutes drive from Tarbet Lodge. Also worth a visit is the Natural History Centre, near the Ardnamurchan distillery, which has an excellent coffee shop and gift shop as well as a display of local information which is free entry. Dark night skies for star gazing and stunning sunsets make this area special.

GETTING AROUND

Public transport is limited but there are many walking routes in particular around the Ariundle Oakwoods at Strontian and over on the Moidart side of Loch Shiel. Cruises on Loch Shiel leaving from Acharacle pier go to Glenfinnan with return by bus. The ferry service to Tobermory from Kilchoan is run by Caledonian MacBrayne and it is possible to do a circular tour to Mull returning via the Fishnish to Lochaline ferry taking your car. Many of the roads around this area of the Western Highlands are single track with passing places, leaflets are available at Tarbet Lodge for driving on these roads.
